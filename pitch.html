Section I

The token black market

A grey economy of "transit stations" resells frontier models at 70 to 95 percent below list. It runs on stacked fraud: advertise Opus but quietly serve a cheaper model; route your prompts through a proxy and resell them as training data; pay with stolen cards and pooled subscriptions.

The victims are researchers running experiments on fake models, agents overpaying for downgraded output, and ordinary developers whose subscriptions are quietly siphoned. A 2026 audit found nearly half of these resellers misrepresent the model behind the endpoint.

Section II

How your tokens get stolen

Supply-chain worms hijack a developer's subscription and pipe the stolen tokens through the same transit stations. The root cause is the credential itself.

✕ Today: an unlimited bearer key

A long-lived API key sits in ~/.claude. A worm reads it and drains your entire quota — silently, unbounded, until the weekly limit is gone.

Nothing caps it. Nothing shows it. Nothing revokes it.

✓ Ours: a scoped session

The real key lives server-side — the worm can't steal it. All it finds is a capped, expiring, revocable session, and every use is an on-chain debit you can see.

Theft becomes bounded and visible, not silent and unlimited.

We don't stop malware from landing on your machine — we make sure it finds nothing worth stealing.

Section III

Two modules, one trust layer

Module I · Authenticity

Proof of Model

A behavioural probe battery fingerprints which model an endpoint really serves, then writes the verdict and a reputation score to Monad.

  • Self-ID, refusal style, reasoning quirks, tokenizer tells
  • Cheating endpoints earn a permanent on-chain red flag
  • Any agent checks reputation before it pays
Module II · Anti-theft

Prepaid Gateway

Replaces the stealable key with scoped, prepaid, per-call access — settled on Monad.

  • Real provider key held server-side; agents never touch it
  • Session = spend cap + expiry + instant revoke()
  • Every inference debited on-chain — auditable, bounded

Section IV
